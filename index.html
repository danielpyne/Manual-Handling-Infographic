Manual Handling for Hair Professionals

Stay safe at work by following best practices when lifting, reaching, and moving equipment in the salon.

Legislation Overview

The UK Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992 (MHOR) require employers to avoid hazardous manual handling where possible, assess the risk of injury, and reduce the risk by implementing safe systems of work. Employees must follow training and use provided equipment correctly.

  • Employers should provide training and equipment such as trolleys or lifting aids.
  • Employees should assess loads before lifting and request assistance if needed.
  • Risk assessments should be documented and regularly reviewed.

Key Tips

Lift with Legs

Bend your knees, not your back, when picking up heavy equipment like dryers or chairs.

Keep Close

Hold tools and products close to your body to reduce strain on your back and shoulders.

Avoid Twisting

Turn your whole body instead of twisting at the waist when moving around the salon.

Use Assistance

Ask for help or use a trolley when lifting or transporting heavy items like product boxes.

Handling Different Objects

Boxes of Products

Test the weight first. If too heavy, split the contents or team lift. Keep the box close to your body.

Salon Chairs

Lower the chair height before moving. Push rather than pull and avoid lifting alone.

Shampoo Basins

Adjust the basin height before use. If moving, empty water first and ask for help.

Trolleys & Kits

Place heavier items on lower shelves. Push the trolley with both hands to avoid twisting your back.
